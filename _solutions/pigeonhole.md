Among any \(n+1\) integers chosen from \(\{1,2,\dots,2n\}\), two are consecutive.
Partition into \(n\) pairs \(\{1,2\},\{3,4\},\dots,\{2n-1,2n\}\).
Placing \(n+1\) choices into \(n\) holes forces two into one pair. \(\square\)
